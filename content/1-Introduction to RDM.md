Research data management (RDM) is the process and coverall term for organising, managing and saving your research data. 

Research data takes on many different formats and media types. It is a lot more than your survey results or interviews.  Your research data can be your library serach strings, notes from a text, annotations from text and on and on. We need to keep and organise this data as this helps your build and direct your research. 
In the course of your dissertaion you will be required to fill out a Data management planning document. This plan covers how you will name and orgainse your files, what data you anticpate saving, where you will save it and for how long it needs to be saved, and the future use and ownership of the data collected. 

The content of this guide will help you complete the data management planning documentation, create file and folder naming conventions, identify how and where you will save your data and the copyright and ethical considerations that you will need to consider to complete your disseration.
